How to Pass the CHC Exam in 2026: The Complete Study Guide

The CHC exam from HCCA tests your knowledge across 7 domains of healthcare compliance. With a pass rate hovering around 65%, preparation is everything. This guide breaks down exactly what to study, how to structure your prep timeline, and which resources actually work.

The exam consists of 120 multiple-choice questions (100 scored) over 2 hours. The biggest domains — Compliance Program Administration (19%) and Monitoring, Auditing & Internal Reporting (18%) — should consume the largest portion of your study time. Focus on the OIG Seven Elements, the Federal Sentencing Guidelines, and the 60-Day Overpayment Rule.

Most successful candidates study for 4-8 weeks, dedicating 30-60 minutes daily to practice questions. Passive reading of textbooks is far less effective than active question-based practice with immediate feedback. Start with scenario-based questions early — the CHC exam is heavily application-based, not recall.

Exam Content

CHC Exam Domains Explained: What You'll Actually Be Tested On

The CHC Detailed Content Outline (DCO) defines exactly what HCCA tests. Here's what each domain covers and what to prioritize.

Compliance Program Administration (19%) covers the OIG's Seven Elements, compliance committee structure, the compliance officer's role, risk assessments, and work plan development. Know the Federal Sentencing Guidelines inside and out.

Monitoring, Auditing & Reporting (18%) tests your knowledge of audit methodology, compliance monitoring techniques, the 60-Day Overpayment Rule, voluntary self-disclosure protocols, and internal reporting mechanisms.

Investigations & Remedial Measures (16%) covers investigation procedures, root cause analysis, corrective action plans, and documentation requirements. Expect scenario-based questions about discovering potential violations.

Screening & Evaluation (6%) — the smallest domain — covers OIG exclusion screening, background checks, and vendor due diligence. Don't skip it; those 6 questions could make or break your score.

Key Regulations

5 Laws Every CHC Candidate Must Know Cold

The CHC exam doesn't just test whether you've heard of these laws — it tests whether you can apply them to real workplace scenarios. Here are the five you cannot afford to get wrong.

1. The Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS) — Prohibits offering, paying, soliciting, or receiving anything of value to induce referrals for services covered by federal healthcare programs. Know the safe harbors.

2. The Stark Law — Prohibits physician self-referrals for designated health services. Unlike AKS, Stark is a strict liability statute. Know the exceptions, especially in-office ancillary services and fair market value.

3. The False Claims Act (FCA) — The government's primary tool for combating healthcare fraud. Know the qui tam (whistleblower) provisions and treble damages.

4. HIPAA Privacy & Security Rules — Covered entities, business associates, minimum necessary standard, breach notification requirements, and enforcement.

5. The 60-Day Overpayment Rule — Healthcare providers must report and return identified overpayments within 60 days or face FCA liability. This is heavily tested.
